### Notes

Prepared a verbal mini-demo of one bug from Exercise 4 to walk through with a partner. Picked Bug 4 (the string-vs-number temperature bug) because it's the most interesting: no error message, runs fine, only fails on specific inputs.

**The story arc, in five beats**

1. **What was the bug:** Entering `9` returned "It's hot outside!"
2. **How I found it:** Tested with multiple inputs. `90` worked, `9` didn't. Works for some inputs and fails for others is the signature of a comparison or type issue, not a typo or missing variable.
3. **What was the fix:** Wrap `prompt()` in `Number()` to cast the string to a number before comparison.
4. **How I know it works:** Tested again with `9`, `90`, `50`, `100`. All three branches of the if/else fire correctly.
5. **First thing to try in a similar bug:** Check the type of each value going into a comparison. `typeof variable` in the console is a one-second check that catches string-vs-number issues every time.

### What I learned

Explaining a bug out loud forces you to separate symptom from cause from fix. If any of those three slots are fuzzy in your head, you'll stumble at that beat. The structure (symptom, diagnosis, fix, verification, general principle) doubles as a debugging template: if you can't fill in all five for a bug you just fixed, you didn't really understand it, you just made it stop hurting.
