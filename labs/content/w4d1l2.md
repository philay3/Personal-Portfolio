### Notes

Second exercise: take a partner's problem idea and stress-test it against four questions. The point isn't to shoot it down, it's to surface what's underneath.

My partner's idea was to make Atlas (the learning platform we use in Next Chapter) better by open-sourcing it so the whole cohort could contribute. We worked through:

**Who would use this?**
People in the cohort, mainly. Maybe instructors as a stretch.

**How is this different from something that already exists?**
Most learning platforms are admin-only. Students consume, admins shape. Open-sourcing flips that so users own the improvements.

**Is this a real problem?**
Yes, for students. The frustration with structure was real enough that "Atlas not being structured enough" came up unprompted from another group member's notes too. So it isn't just one person's complaint.

**Could you build a basic version in a few weeks?**
Yes. The MVP would be a community fork with PRs for content corrections plus a small contributor doc.

### What I learned

The four questions are deceptively simple, but they force the conversation to move. "Could you build it in a few weeks?" especially. It kills grand ideas fast and rescues small ones. It also reframed Atlas from "platform that frustrates me" to "platform with a fixable structure problem and an obvious contributor base sitting right there."
