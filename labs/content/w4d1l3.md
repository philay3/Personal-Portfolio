### Notes

For Exercise 3 I picked the legal tech problem from the brainstorm and wrote five user stories in the standard format: As a X, I want to Y, so that Z.

The product idea: a tool to help legal tech sales teams find the right law firms to pitch.

**Story 1. The SDR doing prospecting.**

> As a Legal Tech SDR, I want to filter a database of law firms by specific practice areas (e.g., complex litigation, appellate law) and firm size, so that I can build a highly targeted prospect list of offices most likely to need and afford Westlaw's premium research tools.

**Story 2. The AE bypassing the receptionist.**

> As an Account Executive, I want to access verified direct-dial phone numbers and direct email addresses for key decision-makers (like Managing Partners or Directors of Legal Operations), so that I can bypass front-desk receptionists and pitch my products directly to the people with purchasing power.

**Story 3. The marketer timing the campaign.**

> As a B2B Growth Marketer, I want to automatically receive alerts when a target law firm exhibits buying intent (such as posting job listings for legal researchers or opening a new branch office), so that I can deploy timely, highly relevant ad campaigns when the firm is actively scaling.

**Story 4. The sales ops manager cleaning the CRM.**

> As a Sales Operations Manager, I want the lead generation platform to automatically track attorney movements and update our CRM when a partner changes firms or starts a new practice, so that our sales team does not waste time and daily effort calling on decayed data or dead email addresses.

**Story 5. The strategist watching for vendor renewals.**

> As a Legal Tech Strategist, I want to view a law firm's estimated technology stack and potential legacy contract renewal dates (e.g., knowing if they currently use LexisNexis), so that I can craft competitive displacement campaigns and time my outreach precisely when they are evaluating vendor renewals.

### What I learned

Writing five forced me to think about different people in the same buying chain. The SDR, AE, marketer, sales ops manager, and strategist all want different cuts of the same underlying data, which is a hint about what the product actually needs to be: not a feature, a database with several lenses on top.

Also: Story 5 (competitive displacement timing) is the most ambitious by far. Probably out of scope for an MVP. The first two are the load-bearing ones.
