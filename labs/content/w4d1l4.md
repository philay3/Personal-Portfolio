### Notes

Exercises 4 and 5 collapsed into one artifact: a written MVP scope, narrowed by what's already on the market. Working name: **LegalSight**.

### Product overview

A lightweight, web-based intelligence dashboard that lets B2B legal tech sales teams identify mid-sized law firms, filter by practice area, surface direct contact info for decision-makers, and track basic growth signals.

### Core features (in scope)

**1. Firmographic filtering engine. The "who".**

- Targeted search UI with three filters: practice area (complex litigation, appellate, corporate), firm size (focusing on the "missing middle" of 10-250 employees), and geography.
- Result cards showing firm name, estimated revenue band, website, and HQ location.

**2. Decision-maker enrichment. The "how to reach".**

- Role-based targeting to isolate specific personas (Managing Partner, IT Director, Director of Legal Operations).
- "Reveal contact" action that pulls verified email and direct-dial numbers via a third-party B2B data API (Apollo, Clearbit, similar).

**3. Basic intent signal feed. The "when".**

A simple feed tracking two high-intent signals:

- Hiring surges: recently posted job descriptions for paralegals or legal researchers (case volume growth).
- Footprint expansion: public announcements of newly opened branch offices.

**4. CSV export. The workflow bridge.**

Select enriched contacts, export to CSV. This is the deliberate compromise. It avoids the massive scope creep of native bi-directional CRM syncs while still letting users move data into Salesforce or HubSpot themselves.

### Out of scope (post-MVP)

Cut to keep the build tight:

- Native bi-directional CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot).
- In-app email sequencing or VoIP dialing.
- Predictive AI lead scoring based on historical win/loss data.
- Comprehensive competitive tech-stack mapping (e.g., detecting whether a firm currently uses LexisNexis).

### Proposed technical architecture

- **Frontend:** React, bootstrapped with Vite for instant server start and fast HMR.
- **Styling:** Tailwind CSS alongside vanilla CSS where strict control over DOM elements matters.
- **Backend / API layer:** Node.js (Express) routing requests to external data providers, handling rate limits, protecting API keys.
- **Database:** Lightweight PostgreSQL (Supabase or similar) for auth, session state, and saved searches.
- **Deployment:** Vercel.

### What I learned

The hardest part of this exercise wasn't writing what's in, it was deciding what to cut. CRM integration was the obvious first thing to want and the obvious first thing to drop. CSV export is uglier but it works, and it lets the build stay focused on the actual unsolved problem: finding the right firms and the right people inside them.

Competitive research mostly confirmed the gap. The big incumbents (ZoomInfo, Apollo, ordinary sales intel platforms) treat law firms as just another vertical. None of them filter by practice area the way an SDR pitching Westlaw actually needs to. That's the wedge.
