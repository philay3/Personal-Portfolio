### Notes

Exercise 2 was confirming the site behaves at three viewports plus a real-device test. Used Chrome DevTools' device toolbar for the three emulated widths, then opened the live site on my actual phone.

### Phone (375px, iPhone SE emulation)

- Text readable without zooming.
- No horizontal scroll.
- Nav links stack and stay tappable.
- Portrait scales down without breaking layout.
- Project card image fits inside the card.
- Tap targets (links and buttons) are at least 44px tall.

One issue caught here: the nav was wrapping awkwardly at 375px, with the four links on two unbalanced lines. Adjusted the `flex-wrap` and `gap` on `.site-nav` so the wrapping looks intentional.

### Tablet (768px)

- Layout adapts cleanly between mobile and desktop.
- No awkward in-between state where the mobile styles haven't kicked in but the desktop spacing is too generous.
- Project card sits at a comfortable width.

This was the cleanest viewport. 768px sits above the 700px breakpoint, so it gets the desktop styling with no special-casing.

### Desktop (1200px+)

- Content doesn't stretch the full screen width.
- Max-width on the content container keeps reading length comfortable.
- Hero image and headings don't feel lost in empty space.

The 1100px `--max-width` on each section is doing the heavy lifting here. Without it, body copy on a wide monitor would stretch into long unreadable lines.

### Real device test

After DevTools passed, I opened philanthony.dev on my actual phone over cellular. Two things only a real device catches:

1. Tap-and-hold behavior on links. DevTools doesn't simulate this well.
2. Real load time on cellular vs simulated network throttling. Loaded in just under 2 seconds, which is fine for a media-light site.

### What I learned

DevTools is good for catching layout problems. It's bad at catching feel problems. The nav wrap issue showed up in DevTools, but the "is this nav actually tappable" question only resolves on a real phone in my hand. Next project, I'm testing on a real phone from Day 1, not at the end.

The other learn: max-width and flexbox do most of the responsive work for free. The one media query is the patch where they aren't enough. Most of the layout being fluid means I don't need to babysit it across breakpoints.
